# Water, Food & Hygiene in Emergencies

## WATER — Finding and Purifying

### How Much Water Do You Need?
- Minimum survival: 1 liter per day (in shade, minimal activity)
- Recommended: 2-3 liters per day
- Hot climate or physical activity: 4+ liters per day
- Dehydration kills faster than starvation — water is ALWAYS the priority

### Signs of Dehydration
- Mild: Thirst, dry mouth, dark yellow urine, headache
- Moderate: Very dry mouth, little or no urine, dizziness, rapid heartbeat
- Severe: No urine, sunken eyes, confusion, rapid weak pulse, unconsciousness
- Children: Cry without tears, dry diaper for 3+ hours, sunken soft spot on head
TREATMENT: Sip small amounts of water frequently — do NOT gulp large amounts at once (causes vomiting).

### Finding Water
- Rainwater: Safest natural source — collect with any clean container, cloth, or tarp
- Dew: Wipe grass or metal surfaces with a cloth in early morning, wring into container
- Follow animals, birds, and insects — they lead to water
- Dig in dry riverbeds — water often sits just below the surface
- Green vegetation in dry areas indicates underground water
- Condensation: Tie a plastic bag around a leafy tree branch — water collects in the bag
- Air conditioning units: Drain water is relatively clean
- Hot water heaters: Contains stored water
- Toilet tanks (NOT bowls): Water is clean if no chemical cleaners were used

### Water Purification Methods (No Equipment)

#### Boiling (Most Reliable)
1. Bring water to a ROLLING BOIL
2. Boil for 1 minute (3 minutes at high altitude above 2000m)
3. Let cool before drinking
4. Boiling kills ALL bacteria, viruses, and parasites

#### Solar Disinfection (SODIS)
1. Fill clear plastic bottle (PET) with water
2. Shake for 20 seconds to increase oxygen
3. Place in direct sunlight for 6-8 hours (or 2 days if cloudy)
4. UV light destroys most pathogens
5. Works best with clear water — filter cloudy water first through cloth

#### Cloth Filtration (Removes Particles, NOT Bacteria)
1. Layer cloth over container opening — multiple layers improve filtering
2. Pour water through slowly
3. This removes dirt, debris, and some parasites
4. STILL NEEDS boiling or chemical treatment after filtering

#### Chemical Treatment
- Household bleach (unscented, 5-6% sodium hypochlorite): 2 drops per liter, wait 30 minutes
- Iodine: 5 drops per liter of clear water, wait 30 minutes
- Water purification tablets: Follow package instructions
NOTE: Chemical treatment does NOT remove chemical contaminants — only kills organisms.

### Water to AVOID
- Floodwater (contaminated with sewage, chemicals, debris)
- Standing water near dead animals or sewage
- Water with chemical smell or unusual color
- Saltwater (causes faster dehydration)
- Radiator fluid or antifreeze (DEADLY poison)

## FOOD SAFETY IN EMERGENCIES

### Safe Food Priorities (Eat First → Last)
1. Perishable food (meat, dairy, fresh food) — eat within hours if no refrigeration
2. Canned food — safe for years, even without labels
3. Dried food (rice, pasta, beans, lentils) — very long shelf life
4. Sealed packages (crackers, bars, sealed snacks)

### Opening Cans Without a Can Opener
1. Rub the top of the can back and forth on rough concrete or a flat rock
2. The friction wears through the lid seal
3. Once the seal breaks, pry open with a knife, spoon, or fingers
4. Check contents — if it smells bad, looks discolored, or is fizzy, do NOT eat

### Food Safety Without Refrigeration
- Cooked food is safe for 2-4 hours at room temperature, then MUST be reheated or discarded
- Salt preserves meat: pack raw meat heavily in salt, it lasts days to weeks
- Smoking/drying meat over fire kills bacteria and extends shelf life
- Sugar preserves fruit (jam-making principle)
- Vinegar or lemon juice preserves vegetables
- Keep food covered, elevated, and away from insects and animals

### Signs of Unsafe Food
- Unusual smell or taste
- Mold (except on hard cheese — cut 1 inch around mold, rest is safe)
- Swollen or leaking cans
- Bubbling or fizzing when opened
- Slimy texture on meat or vegetables
When in doubt, DON'T eat it. Starvation takes weeks; food poisoning can kill in days.

### Food Poisoning Treatment
1. Stay hydrated — small sips of water or oral rehydration solution
2. Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS): 1 liter clean water + 6 teaspoons sugar + ½ teaspoon salt
3. Rest
4. Do NOT take anti-diarrhea medication — the body needs to expel the toxin
5. Seek medical help if: bloody diarrhea, fever over 38.5°C/101°F, inability to keep water down, symptoms lasting more than 3 days

## HYGIENE — PREVENTING DISEASE

### Why Hygiene Matters in War Zones
Disease kills more people in prolonged conflicts than weapons. Cholera, dysentery, typhoid, and infections spread through poor sanitation.

### Hand Washing (Most Important Hygiene Action)
Wash hands: before eating, after using toilet, before treating wounds, after touching contaminated material.
- With soap and water: scrub for 20 seconds, between fingers, under nails
- No soap: use ash from a fire (alkaline, has antibacterial properties)
- No water: use alcohol-based sanitizer, or even sand/dirt and rinse when water becomes available

### Toilet/Sanitation Without Plumbing
1. Dig a latrine: hole at least 30cm (1 foot) deep, 30 meters from any water source
2. Cover waste with soil each time
3. Place latrine DOWNHILL from water source and living area
4. If no digging possible: use sealed bags, containers, or buckets with tight lids
5. Wash hands EVERY time after using the toilet — this prevents disease spread

### Wound Hygiene
- Clean wounds daily with cleanest water available
- Change dressings at least once daily
- Watch for infection signs: increasing redness, swelling, pus, fever
- Honey applied to wounds has natural antibacterial properties

### Dental Emergencies
- Toothache: Rinse with warm salt water, apply clove oil if available (natural anesthetic)
- Knocked out tooth: Place in milk or hold in cheek (saliva preserves it) — can be replanted within 1 hour
- Broken tooth: Rinse mouth, cover sharp edges with wax, gum, or soft food to protect tongue
- Abscess (swollen, painful gum): Rinse with salt water, do NOT press or pop — seek dental care

### Insect and Pest Prevention
- Sleep under netting if available
- Cover food and water containers
- Check shoes/clothing before wearing (scorpions, spiders)
- Burn or bury garbage to reduce fly breeding
- Standing water breeds mosquitoes — drain or cover any water containers
